Mackenzie Kean's S22 Co-op Work Term Report

Introduction

For the past 8 months I have been working as an IT technician at the Canadian Border Services Agency. This has been my second co-op work term with CBSA and I have now worked at the agency for over 12 months full time. I was also given the opportunity to work for CBSA part time during the Fall 2022 semester so it feels like I have never left. This work term has been another great co-op experience and I have been given many new opportunities at work to utilize and further develop my skills. This term was also unique as I was able to work with new team members and assist them with training and onboarding which I believe demonstrates my knowledge and how far I have come since I started working at CBSA. These past 12 months have been the first time in my career that I have had the opportunity to develop code in a team setting and I have significantly improved my development abilities in the process. I have learned react since my first term with CBSA and have been given the opportunity to use it when creating a prototype user interface demo for an upcoming project that my team was tasked with. In addition, I have been able to further improve my SQL skills and acquire experience in data analysis using tools such as excel to create and share reports. I am also happy to say that I have had discussions with my supervisor about returning to CBSA after I graduate and I am hoping to make this a reality in the near future.

About CBSA

The CBSA is the Canadian Border Services Agency. According to their website, https://www.cbsa-asfc.gc.ca/menu-eng.html, “The Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) facilitates the flow of legitimate travellers and trade. The agency also enforces more than 100 acts and regulations that keep our country and Canadians safe.”. Places you will see CBSA employees include border crossings between Canada and the United States, International airports, and marine ports. However, none of these places are where you would find me during a given day at work. I am part of the Travellers Systems Division of CBSA and have been working remotely during my work term. CBSA has lots of technical employees who are not on the front lines but ensure that existing systems used by the agency are working properly to help protect our nation's borders. My colleagues work very hard not only to maintain these systems but also to improve and innovate upon them such that the agency can meet their objectives more effectively.

Job Description

During my time with CBSA I worked on a variety of different tasks and with a variety of different technologies. My job title as per my job description is “IT Technician” and at first it felt like I was entering more of a junior development role but I soon discovered that the fun amount of variety that exists between the various assigned tasks in this role would make a job title of junior programmer inaccurate. This role included programming, analysis, learning new technologies, testing, and design. I think a good way to describe it would be more software engineering than programming as senior members on my team would frequently point out different poor design choices in code and ask my colleague and I to think of a better design to solve the problem. We were prompted to think about how the problem was being solved and whether or not our solutions were optimal rather than simply being sufficient.

Every morning at work started with the same ritual, the scrum email. Each member of my team involved in development tasks would write a scrum email outlining what we worked on the day prior, what we plan on completing today, and whether there are any blockers preventing us from completing our goals. I enjoyed these because they helped me set goals for myself and track my progress throughout the week while also staying in the loop with what my teammates are working on. It also provided me with a very easy opportunity to alert my team if I was every stuck or confused about something. Starting out and even still today there are things that I am still learning where my lack of knowledge stops me from finishing the task and I have to ask for help. I generally do not have a problem with asking for help but providing a section to do so directly from the scrum emails makes asking for help much less intimidating.

My team also took the time to meet quite frequently during my time with CBSA this summer. In general, we have 2 types of meetings. The first type of meetings are the daily development scrum meetings. These meetings are where the developers and team leader of my team meet to discuss one of the large development tasks that we are working on for roughly 30 minutes each morning. A lot of the information discussed in these meetings is specific to the project and thus I am not able to talk about it here. Part of working in this role involves working with sensitive information sometimes. Before I started I had to earn a security clearance from the government as a condition of my employment. On the subject of the scrum meetings, their general structure is mostly that of a “round table” where people would go around and give updates on their development activities. As a student I was not assigned a great deal of work on the large development task these meetings were about but because of the learning experience I appreciated the meetings where I was observing just as much as the meetings where I was giving updates about my work.

The next type of meeting my team had were weekly meetings. These meetings involved my entire team and were much longer than our daily scrum meetings at around an hour in length. The goal for these meetings was to discuss all the various tasks each person is working on and if any issues arose during the week. It was not uncommon for members of the team to discuss various possible solutions to a problem during portions of these meetings. Often times upon touching base with the team various team members would realize that other more specific meetings needed to be scheduled and they would schedule these other meetings afterwards. In my department it is quite common for people to work in a matrix structure with various teams collaborating on tasks or certain individuals from different teams lending their skills to ours. The weekly team meetings were often a place where I noticed my coworkers exchanging ideas for who might be best to collaborate with for a certain task based on their experience. Weekly meetings always ended with a round table as well.

As for the specific tasks I was assigned it was a split between research, programming, analysis tasks, and technical writing. When I first started with CBSA I spent time reading through system use case documentation for the system I would be working on. These system use cases helped me understand the way that the system works and responds to certain scenarios. Variations of what happens during different flows much like the fully dressed use cases I remember preparing last semester in CIS*3750.

This term, I was also given the opportunity to take multiple udemy courses for React and AWS cloud technologies which helped improve my skills as a developer and introduce me further to the capabilities of cloud computing technologies such as those found on AWS. Not only was I given the opportunity to work on completing these courses but I was also given contacts of colleagues in my division which were experienced in React and AWS which gave me the chance to seek help and ask questions when I needed to.
